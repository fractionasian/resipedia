    8.2 Define the term and give units for: [G] Activity, apparent activity, specific activity, air-kerma rate constant and reference air-kerma rate¶

    Activity is defined as the number of disintegrations per unit time. Usually denoted as A. The SI unit for activity is the becquerel (Bq), defined as one disintegration per second (dps). In radiation therapy, a more common unit of activity is the curie (Ci), defined as: 1 Ci = 3.7 x 1010 Bq

    Apparent activity (of a brachytherapy source) is defined as the activity of a bare point source of the same nuclide, that produces the same exposure rate at 1 m as the source to be specified. It is determined by dividing the measured exposure rate at 1 m with the exposure rate constant of the unfiltered source at 1 m. It is a measure of the strength of a source, if the source is calibrated in terms of exposure rate at 1 m.

    Specific activity is the activity per unit mass of a radionuclide. Originally 1 Ci was the decay rate of 1 g of radium, however the actual decay rate of radium has been revised and so the specific activity of radium is slightly less than 1 Ci/g.
